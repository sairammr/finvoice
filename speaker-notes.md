---

## Slide 1 — The Problem (~25s)

"Invoice factoring today is **broken**. Legacy rails optimize for speed of capital, not legitimacy of claims.

Three core pain points:
1. **No debtor consent** — invoices get tokenized unilaterally, no verification.
2. **Privacy violation** — business relationships are exposed on-chain for anyone to see.
3. **Terrible UX** — MetaMask, dApps, complex flows that nobody outside crypto uses.

This $3.1 trillion market still runs on faxed PDFs and blind trust."

---

## Slide 2 — The Solution (~25s)

"**Hedsup.Finance** unlocks a new economy where private assets meet public liquidity, powered by AI agents on Flare.

Invoices live on a **private chain** — debtor identities and payment history never leave. An AI agent scores credit risk and bridges only what matters — risk grade, yield, confidence — to a **public marketplace** where anyone can fund invoices.

The key primitive: **PDF onramp**. Debtors approve invoices through a regular PDF — OTP-verified, no wallet, no dApp. One click tokenizes the invoice and kicks off the entire pipeline. TradFi UX, DeFi rails."

---

## Slide 3 — User Flow (~20s)

"Here's the end-to-end flow:

Supplier creates an invoice, a PDF is generated with an embedded approval link. The debtor clicks approve in the PDF — that single click tokenizes the invoice on our privacy node. The AI agent scores it, grades A through D, mints an attestation, and it crosses to the public marketplace. A funder purchases the token, and the supplier gets **instant settlement**.

Green boxes are user actions, grey boxes are fully automated."

---

## Slide 4 — Architecture (~15s)

"Two-layer architecture. The **Privacy Node** holds debtor identity, payment history, full AI reasoning, jurisdiction, and invoice terms — none of this leaves the private layer.

The **Public L1** only sees risk grade, yield APY, face value, maturity, and a PDF hash.

AI sees everything. Public sees only pricing signals."

## Slide 7 — Where Yield Comes From (~20s)

"Where does the yield come from? The **discount is the yield**.

A supplier has a $100k invoice and needs cash now. They list it on Hedsup. A funder buys it at a discount — say $97,900. At maturity, the debtor pays the full $100k. The spread — $2,100 over 30 days — annualizes to **26.1% APY**.

Real invoices, real payments, real yield. No token emissions, no ponzinomics. Platform takes **0.3% at settlement**."

## Slide 8 — The Economics (~15s)

"Breaking down the economics with a Grade A, $100k, 30-day invoice:

Purchase price $97,900 — yield $2,100 — that's **26.1% APY**. And this is the *lowest* risk tier.

Higher risk grades offer even more: Grade B goes 38–58%, Grade C 71–113%, Grade D 116% and above. All backed by real commercial obligations, guaranteed by institutions."
